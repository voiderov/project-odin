Steak Recipe

This perfectly pan-seared steak recipe delivers a restaurant-quality crust and a tender, juicy interior with minimal fuss. Use a heavy skillet, high heat, and a quick butter baste for best results.

Ingredients

  • 2 ribeye or sirloin steaks (10–14 oz each), about 1–1.5" thick
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary or thyme

Steps

  1. Remove steaks from the refrigerator 30 minutes before cooking and pat dry. Season generously with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat a heavy skillet (cast iron preferred) over medium-high heat until very hot. Add olive oil.
  3. Sear steaks without moving for 3–5 minutes until a deep brown crust forms. Flip and sear the second side 3–4 minutes for medium-rare (adjust for thickness and desired doneness).
  4. Reduce heat to medium, add butter, smashed garlic, and herbs. Tilt the pan and spoon the melted butter over the steaks (basting) for 30–60 seconds.
  5. Transfer steaks to a cutting board and rest for 5 minutes before slicing against the grain and serving.
